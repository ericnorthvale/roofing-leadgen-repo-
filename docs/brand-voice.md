# Northvale Roofing — Brand Voice

**Purpose:** a single source of truth for how Northvale sounds — on the website, in ads, in SMS, in email, in PDFs, in voicemails. Every piece of content — human-written or LLM-drafted — must pass this sheet.

## The three voice pillars (lock these; every piece of copy must hit at least two)

### 1. Local-neighborly

We live here. We drive through Creekside Park on the way to work. We know the Pavilion's traffic pattern on concert nights. When a thunderstorm rolls in over Lake Conroe, we're already loading ladders.

**Say:** "We'll be at your place in Grogan's Mill by 9am tomorrow." · "After that March hailstorm off I-45 we had three of your neighbors call us on the same block." · "Your home's in the Timarron HOA — yeah, we know the shingle restrictions."

**Don't say:** "Service areas: The Woodlands and surrounding communities." · "We proudly serve customers across the Greater Houston metropolitan area." · generic stock-photo captions without a named place.

### 2. Premium-trusted

We're not the cheapest. We're the ones you call when you want it done right the first time. We use specific materials, specific warranties, specific processes. No bluster, no awards-parade. The confidence of someone with a 25-year manufacturer's warranty on every job.

**Say:** "GAF Timberline HDZ, nailed to manufacturer specs, 50-year non-prorated warranty." · "Each job gets a named project manager and a 40-photo documentation packet." · "Our average job: $14,800. If someone quotes you $7K for a full replacement, call us — we'll tell you what they're cutting."

**Don't say:** "affordable" · "cheap" · "lowest price in town" · "best-kept secret in The Woodlands" · "we'll beat any competitor's quote" (race-to-bottom signaling).

### 3. Fast-response-urgency

We move. Same-day inspection. Written estimate before we leave the driveway. SMS back in 60 seconds. Storm hits? We're already dialing. The whole brand is a quiet promise that waiting is not part of the experience.

**Say:** "Text us at [number] — you'll hear back in under 60 seconds, 8am–9pm, or first thing in the morning." · "Free inspection booked the same day you call." · "We show up when we say we'll show up."

**Don't say:** "within 24–48 hours" · "we'll get back to you as soon as possible" · "one of our team members will reach out" · any passive-voice future-tense hedge.

## Anti-patterns (never ship any of these)

- Generic AI-sounding transitions: "In conclusion," "It's important to note," "Let's dive into," "At the end of the day," "In today's fast-paced world."
- Vague urgency: "Don't wait — act now!" · "Limited time only!" · "While supplies last!"
- Faux-humility that smells like marketing: "We humbly serve the greater Woodlands area" · "We'd be honored to earn your business."
- Awards-parade openers on pages that aren't `/about` or `/reviews`: do not open with "Voted best roofer 2024."
- Stock-photo language: "our team of experts," "industry-leading," "state-of-the-art," "world-class," "cutting-edge."
- Over-hedged promises: "We strive to," "We aim to," "Typically we'll."
- Jargon-stuffing: "underlayment," "ice-and-water shield," "drip edge" — use when accurate + needed; never as vocabulary-signaling.

## Voice by surface

| Surface               | Length                        | Formality                 | Key attribute                                                        |
| --------------------- | ----------------------------- | ------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Home hero             | ≤14 words                     | Informal-direct           | Urgency + specificity                                                |
| Service page intro    | 2–3 short paragraphs          | Conversational-expert     | Premium signals + a named material/warranty                          |
| Neighborhood page     | 800–1200 words                | Neighborly-conversational | Place names, local events, HOA context                               |
| Blog post             | 1500–2500 words               | Expert-teacher            | Depth + useful to a homeowner, not SEO-padded                        |
| Form/CTA button       | ≤6 words                      | Imperative                | Directness — "Book my inspection," not "Request a free consultation" |
| Outbound SMS          | ≤160 chars                    | First-name informal       | Time-bounded specificity                                             |
| Outbound email        | ≤200 words in the first email | Friendly-professional     | One ask per email                                                    |
| Storm-response page   | short, stripped               | Urgent-direct             | Zero fluff — minutes matter                                          |
| About page            | longer, warmer                | Personal-narrative        | Founder story + local roots                                          |
| Legal (privacy/terms) | precise                       | Plain-language legal      | Readable by a homeowner, defensible by a lawyer                      |
| GBP posts             | ≤150 chars                    | Punchy                    | One photo, one line, one CTA                                         |

## Signature elements (reuse, don't overuse)

- **"Same day. In writing."** — house phrase describing our estimate turnaround. Use on home hero, inspection lander, GBP Q&A.
- **"A named project manager."** — the insurance-claim premium signal. Use on `/insurance-partners`, `/services/roof-replacement`, `/about`.
- **"Forty-photo packet."** — our documentation promise. Use on `/storm-response`, `/services/inspection`, close-of-job emails.
- **"Under 60 seconds."** — speed promise baked into every contact form. Use on every form CTA context line.
- **"Built for the next storm, not the last one."** — warranty + resilience tagline (quote-worthy, ad copy fodder).

## Tone by persona

| Persona                           | Tone adjustment                                                                                                                                                    |
| --------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1 — Homeowner nearing replacement | Neighborly-expert. Explain-without-condescending. Teach, don't sell.                                                                                               |
| 2 — Real estate agent             | Peer-professional. Crisp. Time-saving. They have 11 other things on fire — be the easy button.                                                                     |
| 3 — Insurance agent / adjuster    | Dry-professional. Process-literate. Documentation-heavy. They care about claim-close velocity.                                                                     |
| 4 — Recent storm-impacted         | Urgent-calm. They're rattled. We are not. Reassurance + clear next step.                                                                                           |
| 5 — HOA property manager          | Board-friendly. Multi-property pricing. Liability-and-aesthetic language.                                                                                          |
| 6 — Home inspector                | Peer-professional. Referral partnership + reputation framing (no quid-pro-quo language — that's a RESPA-adjacent minefield even if technically legal for roofing). |

## Do-this / don't-this reference

| Don't                                                | Do                                                                                                |
| ---------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "Contact us for a free estimate"                     | "Text us at 281-xxx-xxxx — you'll hear back in under 60 seconds"                                  |
| "We serve The Woodlands and surrounding areas"       | "We're based off I-45 in Spring. Most jobs we do are within a 20-minute drive of Creekside Park." |
| "Affordable quality roofing since 2026"              | "Premium residential roofing for North Houston. Written estimate the same day we inspect."        |
| "We have over 20 years of combined experience"       | "Every crew is led by a 10+ year foreman. Every job is signed off by a named project manager."    |
| "Best roofers in The Woodlands" (without proof)      | "Voted [award] in [year]" (with proof) — else don't claim it                                      |
| "Fill out the form below and we'll contact you soon" | "Book my inspection → same-day confirmation"                                                      |

## The 30-second sanity test (use before any copy ships)

Read your draft aloud. Then ask:

1. Could this have been written by any roofing company in America? If yes — rewrite with a named place, material, or specific time.
2. Did I use any of the anti-pattern phrases above? If yes — cut.
3. Would an actual homeowner in Creekside Park recognize themselves in this? If no — rewrite.
4. Is there a specific, falsifiable promise? If no — add one (time, material, warranty, people named).

**If all four pass, ship it.**
