If you live anywhere in the Houston hail belt, you've probably heard a roofer mention
"Class-4" or "impact-resistant" shingles. Here's a straight answer on what they are
and whether the upgrade earns its cost.

## What "Class-4" actually means

Impact resistance is graded by a lab test called **UL 2218**, where a steel ball is
dropped on the shingle to simulate hail. Class-4 is the highest rating — the shingle
survives a 2-inch steel ball without cracking the mat. It's a real, measurable
standard, not a marketing label. Class-4 shingles use a tougher mat and modified
asphalt, so they resist bruising and granule loss from hail far better than standard
architectural shingles.

## The insurance angle (the part that often pays for it)

This is where Texas homeowners benefit most: **many Texas insurers offer a premium
discount on the wind/hail portion of your policy** for a verified Class-4 roof. The
size of the discount varies by carrier and policy, so the honest move is to **ask
your insurer what they'd credit** before you decide. Keep the manufacturer's Class-4
certification — your carrier will want it on file.

Over the life of the roof, that recurring discount can offset a meaningful share of
the upgrade cost. Whether it fully pays back depends on your premium and how long you
stay in the home — run your own numbers with your insurer's actual discount.

## When the upgrade is worth it

- **You're in a frequent-hail area** (much of NW Houston qualifies).
- **You plan to stay** in the home long enough to collect several years of premium
  discount.
- **You want fewer claims and repairs** — a more durable roof simply takes hail better.

When it's less compelling: if you expect to sell soon, or your carrier offers little
or no discount, the math is weaker. There's no universal right answer — it's a
cost-vs-benefit call for your situation.
