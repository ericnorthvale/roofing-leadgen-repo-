Gulf Coast storms move fast, and the damage they leave on a roof is often invisible
from the ground. If a hail or high-wind event just rolled through your neighborhood,
here's a calm, in-order checklist — and the mistakes that cost homeowners the most.

## First: stay off the roof

A wet or damaged roof is dangerous, and walking it can create damage that
complicates a claim. Do your first look from the ground and the gutters. Leave the
roof-level inspection to someone with fall protection.

## How to tell if your roof actually took a hit

Not every storm causes roof damage worth a claim. These are the honest indicators:

- **Gutters and downspouts:** fresh, round dents on the gutter apron or soft metal.
- **A/C condenser fins:** dings on the unit's coil are a strong hail-size proxy —
  photograph them, they're at ground level.
- **Shingles:** granule loss collecting in gutters beyond the normal shed of an
  aging roof; bruised or fractured shingle mats (best seen up close).
- **Ridge caps and flashing:** lifted or torn caps point to wind exposure.

If you see several of these, a documented inspection is worth it. If you don't, a
claim may be denied — and **a denied claim can be worse than no claim**.

## Document before you call anyone

Photos win claims. Capture dated photos of the gutters, the A/C unit, any interior
ceiling stains, and the storm date. A reputable roofer's inspection adds a
slope-by-slope photo packet aligned to the line items an adjuster uses. See our
[free roof inspection](/services/roof-inspection) and
[storm damage](/services/storm-damage) pages for what thorough documentation looks like.

## Handle the insurance claim the right way

A few rules protect you and keep the claim clean:

- **You file the claim.** A contractor supports and documents; we are not public
  adjusters. (In Texas, that's a separately licensed role — see our
  [insurance claims](/services/insurance-claims) page.)
- **Never accept a "we'll waive your deductible" offer.** That's insurance fraud in
  Texas, with criminal exposure for both you and the contractor. Walk away from anyone
  who suggests it.
- **Don't sign a contract at the door under pressure.** Texas law gives you **three
  days to rescind** a residential roof-repair contract signed at your home after an
  insurance-related solicitation. Take the time.

## What "supplements" are (and why they matter)

When an adjuster's first scope misses code-required items — new decking,
ice-and-water shield, drip edge — a contractor documents those and the carrier pays a
supplement. This is normal and legitimate, and it's where a careful, documentation-
heavy roofer earns their keep on a storm job.
